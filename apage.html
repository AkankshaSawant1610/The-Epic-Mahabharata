महाभारतम्: महागाथा

महाभारत केवल एक महाकाव्य नहीं है; यह जीवन का एक समग्र ग्रंथ है, जो सबसे गहन दार्शनिक अंतर्दृष्टियाँ, अत्यंत गंभीर नैतिक दुविधाएँ और सबसे महान मानवीय भावनाओं को समेटे हुए है। कुरु वंश की दो शाखाओं के बीच हुए ब्रह्मांडीय युद्ध की यह गाथा शाश्वत सत्य प्रस्तुत करती है, जो युगों-युगों तक गूंजती रहती है।

धर्मयात्रा प्रारम्भ

Legendary Figures

Shri Krishna

The supreme personality of godhead who orchestrated the events of the Mahabharata. His teachings in the Bhagavad Gita form the spiritual core of the epic.

"You have the right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions."

Arjuna

The third Pandava brother, peerless archer, and central figure of the Bhagavad Gita. His moral dilemma on the battlefield of Kurukshetra leads to Krishna's divine discourse.

"I desire neither victory, nor kingdom, nor pleasures. Of what avail is kingdom to us?"

Bhishma

The grand patriarch of the Kuru dynasty, renowned for his vow of celibacy and unquestionable loyalty. His death on the bed of arrows is one of the most poignant moments of the epic.

"I have given my word to the Kauravas and must fight for them, though I love the Pandavas."

Draupadi

The fire-born princess who became the wife of all five Pandava brothers. Her humiliation in the Kaurava court becomes the pivotal moment that makes war inevitable.

"Is there no one in this assembly who will defend my honor?"

Duryodhana

The eldest Kaurava who let his envy of the Pandavas consume him. His greed and pride led to the devastating war.

"I would rather die than share this earth with the Pandavas!"

Karna

The tragic hero born to Kunti before her marriage, abandoned at birth, raised by charioteers, and doomed to fight against his own brothers.

"Even if time and death were not on their side, I would still fight the Pandavas alone."
