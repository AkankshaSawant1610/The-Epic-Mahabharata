Bhagavad Gītā
योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा धनञ्जय। सिद्ध्यसिद्ध्योः समो भूत्वा समत्वं योग उच्यते॥
Krishna to Arjuna | Battlefield of Kurukshetra
"yogasthaḥ kuru karmāṇi saṅgaṁ tyaktvā dhanañjaya
siddhyasiddhyoḥ samo bhūtvā samatvaṁ yoga ucyate"

English Translation

"Perform your duty equipoised, O Arjuna, abandoning all attachment to success or failure. Such equanimity is called yoga."

Philosophical Insight

This verse teaches the essence of Karma Yoga - performing actions with detachment from results, maintaining balance in success and failure.

Shānti Parva
धर्म एव हतो हन्ति धर्मो रक्षति रक्षितः। तस्माद्धर्मो न हन्तव्यो मा नो धर्मो हतोऽवधीत्॥
Bhīṣma to Yudhiṣṭhira | Bed of Arrows
"dharma eva hato hanti dharmo rakṣati rakṣitaḥ
tasmāddharmo na hantavyo mā no dharmo hato'vadhīt"

English Translation

"Dharma destroys those who violate it, Dharma protects those who follow it. Therefore never abandon Dharma, lest Dharma destroyed should destroy us."

Philosophical Insight

This emphasizes the fundamental principle that Dharma is both protector and disciplinary force. Upholding righteousness leads to protection, while neglecting it brings destruction.

Udyoga Parva
अपि स्विदर्थं कृतमित्यमन्यत यद्धर्ममास्थाय नमन्नरेन्द्रः। वत्सो यथा वत्सतरीं न जह्याद्धर्मं जहीयात्कथमाग्निहोत्री॥
Krishna to Duryodhana | Peace Mission
"api svidarthaṁ kṛtamityamanyata yaddharmamāsthāya namanarendraḥ
vatso yathā vatsatarīṁ na jahyāddharmam jahīyātkathamāgnihotrī"

English Translation

"O King, don't think that you're achieving anything worthwhile by bending Dharma. Just as a calf never abandons its mother cow, how can one devoted to sacred rites ever abandon Dharma?"

Philosophical Insight

Krishna challenges Duryodhana's rationalization for his actions. The analogy emphasizes Dharma should be as natural and indispensable as the bond between calf and cow.
